# Customer Avatar: The Problem Aware Individual

**Stage of Awareness:** Problem Aware

## A → Who Are They

*   **Name:** David Chen
*   **Gender:** Male
*   **Age:** 38
*   **Job:** Independent Journalist & Substack Publisher
*   **Household Income:** Approximately $75,000 per year, derived from a combination of paid Substack subscriptions and freelance investigative journalism projects. His income is variable and directly tied to the impact and reach of his work.
*   **Marital Status:** Single
*   **Education Level:** Master’s Degree in Journalism from Columbia University

David represents a growing class of independent media professionals who have left mainstream news organizations to pursue a more autonomous and ideologically unconstrained form of journalism. He lives in a moderately sized city in the Midwest, which allows him to maintain a lower cost of living while focusing on national issues. He is deeply embedded in online communities and leverages digital platforms to fund and distribute his work, which primarily focuses on government accountability, corporate malfeasance, and civil liberties. His background in traditional journalism provides him with a rigorous, evidence-based approach, but his independent status allows him to cover stories that larger, advertiser-funded outlets might avoid.

## C → Why Are They

*   **Main Personality Traits:** David is intensely **inquisitive**, driven by a need to understand the underlying systems that govern society. He is a natural **skeptic**, questioning official narratives and demanding evidence to support claims. His work is guided by a **principled** commitment to the truth and the public's right to know. As an independent operator, he is fiercely **self-reliant** and values his autonomy above all else. This independence, however, also fosters a degree of **paranoia**, though it is grounded in a realistic understanding of the risks he faces.

*   **5 Major Values They Hold:**
    1.  **Truth:** He believes in the existence of objective truth and sees its pursuit as the highest calling of his profession.
    2.  **Freedom of Speech:** He holds an absolutist view of the First Amendment, believing that the free exchange of ideas, even controversial ones, is essential for a functioning democracy.
    3.  **Privacy:** He views privacy not as secrecy, but as a fundamental human right that enables autonomy, dissent, and personal dignity.
    4.  **Accountability:** He believes that those in power—whether in government or corporations—must be held accountable for their actions.
    5.  **Justice:** He is driven by a desire to expose injustice and give a voice to the marginalized and oppressed.

*   **2 Major Life Victories:**
    1.  **Exposing a Local Corruption Scandal:** Early in his career, he published a series of articles that exposed a kickback scheme in his city's government, leading to the resignation of two officials and a federal investigation. This victory solidified his belief in the power of investigative journalism.
    2.  **Successfully Launching His Substack:** Leaving the stability of a traditional newsroom was a significant risk. Building a self-sustaining career through direct reader support was a major victory that affirmed his independence and the market for his brand of journalism.

*   **2 Major Life Failures:**
    1.  **A Source Being Compromised:** A source for a sensitive story was identified and fired after David communicated with them over what he believed was a secure channel. This event haunts him and is the primary driver of his search for truly secure communication methods.
    2.  **Inability to Gain Traction on a Major Story:** He spent nearly a year investigating a complex story about corporate lobbying, but was unable to get the key documents needed to publish. The failure taught him a hard lesson about the limits of his resources as an independent journalist.

## D → Smart Market Questions

*   **What keeps them awake at night, eyes open, staring at the ceiling?**
    He replays conversations with sources, worrying if he has done enough to protect their anonymity. He frets about the security of his data, his notes, and his communications. He is acutely aware that a single mistake—a mis-sent email, an unencrypted message, a compromised device—could not only destroy his career but also ruin the lives of the people who trust him. He also worries about the 
broader trends he covers: the erosion of civil liberties, the rise of state surveillance, and the increasing difficulty of holding power to account.

*   **What are they secretly afraid of in life?**
    He is secretly afraid of becoming irrelevant or, worse, being discredited. He fears that a powerful entity he is investigating will launch a smear campaign against him, destroying his reputation and ability to work. He is also afraid of being physically harmed for his work, though he would never admit it publicly. On a deeper level, he fears that his work will ultimately be futile and that the forces of censorship and control he is fighting against are too powerful to be stopped.

*   **What are they angry about, and who are they angry at?**
    He is angry at the systemic corruption and dishonesty he sees in both government and corporate institutions. He is angry at mainstream media outlets that he believes have abdicated their responsibility to challenge power and have become stenographers for the powerful. He is angry at tech companies that build tools of surveillance and profit from the erosion of privacy. Most of all, he is angry at the apathy of the general public, who seem willing to trade their freedom for convenience.

*   **Top 3 frustrations they feel every day:**
    1.  The constant need to self-promote and manage the business side of his Substack, which takes time away from his actual journalistic work.
    2.  The technical challenges of maintaining a secure digital workflow and the constant anxiety about potential vulnerabilities.
    3.  The feeling of being an outsider, isolated from the camaraderie and resources of a traditional newsroom.

*   **Biggest secret desire in life:**
    To break a story so significant that it forces a national conversation and leads to tangible, systemic change—a modern-day Watergate.

*   **Built-in bias to how they make decisions:**
    He has a strong anti-authoritarian bias and is instinctively distrustful of any large, powerful institution, whether public or private. He is more likely to believe a whistleblower or a dissident than an official spokesperson. He values transparency and openness above almost all else.

*   **Common words or language unique to them:**
    He uses terms like "op-sec" (operations security), "threat modeling," "end-to-end encryption," "metadata," "parallel construction," and "chilling effects." He speaks in a precise, evidence-based manner, often citing sources and making careful distinctions.

*   **Top 3 complaints about existing solutions:**
    1.  "WhatsApp and Telegram are owned by large corporations with their own agendas. I don’t trust that my data is truly private, even with end-to-end encryption. Plus, Telegram isn't even encrypted by default."
    2.  "Signal is good, but it still requires a phone number, which creates a link to my real-world identity that I’m not comfortable with, especially for sensitive sources."
    3.  "Email is a disaster. PGP is too complicated for most people to use correctly, and I can’t expect my sources to be security experts."

## E → Going Deep

*   **Top 3 Dominant Negative Emotions:**
    1.  **Anxiety:** A persistent, low-level hum of anxiety about the security of his communications and the safety of his sources.
    2.  **Frustration:** A deep-seated frustration with the state of the world, the difficulty of his work, and the apathy of others.
    3.  **Suspicion:** A default state of suspicion towards anyone or any entity that holds power.

*   **Top 3 Dominant Positive Emotions From Solving This Problem:**
    1.  **Security:** The feeling of profound relief and confidence that comes from knowing his communications are truly private and secure.
    2.  **Empowerment:** The ability to do his job without fear, knowing he can protect his sources and himself.
    3.  **Integrity:** The satisfaction of using a tool that aligns with his core values of privacy and freedom.

*   **Top 3 Beliefs They Hold About The World:**
    1.  Power corrupts, and absolute power corrupts absolutely.
    2.  Information wants to be free, and people have a right to know what their government is doing in their name.
    3.  Individual liberty is fragile and must be actively defended against encroachment from both state and corporate power.

*   **Biggest Lifestyle Desire:**
    To live a life of purpose and impact, free from the constraints of corporate or government control, where he can pursue the truth without fear or compromise.

## F → Purchasing Habits

*   **Top 3 Decision Triggers:**
    1.  **A news event** that highlights government overreach or a major data breach.
    2.  **A recommendation from a trusted source** within the privacy or journalism community (e.g., a tweet from Edward Snowden or an article from the EFF).
    3.  **A direct, personal experience** where a current communication method fails or proves to be insecure.

*   **Prior Purchases For This Pain:**
    He has a subscription to a reputable VPN service (ProtonVPN), uses the Tor browser for sensitive research, and has experimented with various encrypted email services. He has also purchased and read numerous books on privacy and surveillance.

*   **Price Tolerance For Offer:**
    He is willing to pay a premium for a solution that he believes is genuinely secure and aligns with his values. He is accustomed to paying for software and services that support his work. A price point of **$5-$15 per month** for a subscription, or a **one-time fee of up to $100**, would be well within his budget, especially if it is positioned as a tool for professionals who require absolute security. He would see it as a necessary business expense.

*   **Time Horizon Of Solution:**
    He needs a solution that is available and reliable **now**. He is actively looking for better tools and is willing to adopt a new platform immediately if it meets his criteria.

## G → Primary Wants

*   **Wants to gain:** True anonymity and untraceable communication.
*   **Wants to be:** A trusted and respected voice for truth and accountability.
*   **Wants to do:** Conduct his investigative journalism without fear of surveillance or censorship.
*   **Wants to save:** His sources from being exposed and his work from being compromised.
*   **Wants to avoid:** Becoming another victim of surveillance, censorship, or a smear campaign.

## H → Empathy Map

*   **Seeing:** News headlines about new surveillance laws, data breaches, and the persecution of journalists and whistleblowers. Seeing his Substack subscriber count and the engagement with his articles. Seeing the physical and digital security measures he has put in place in his home and on his devices.

*   **Thinking:** "Is this channel secure? Can I trust this person? What are the potential ramifications of publishing this story? How can I verify this information? Am I doing enough to protect my sources?"

*   **Hearing:** Podcasts and interviews with other journalists, privacy experts, and dissidents. The constant notifications from his various devices. The official denials and obfuscations from government spokespeople.

*   **Feeling:** A constant sense of urgency and responsibility. A deep-seated anger at injustice. A profound sense of purpose in his work, but also a feeling of isolation and vulnerability.

*   **Saying:** "We need to talk on an encrypted channel." "Can you verify that?" "What’s your source?" "They are not telling us the whole story." "Privacy is a human right."

*   **Doing:** Writing and publishing articles on his Substack. Encrypting his hard drives. Using a VPN and Tor. Meeting sources in person in discreet locations. Constantly researching new security tools and techniques.
